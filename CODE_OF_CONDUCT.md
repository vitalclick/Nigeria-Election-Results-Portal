# Code of Conduct

OpenBallot Nigeria is a public-interest project. The contributor community
is expected to be welcoming, inclusive, and respectful regardless of
political affiliation, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation,
disability, or background.

## Expected behaviour

- Assume good faith. People come to civic tech from many starting points.
- Critique ideas, not people. Reviews focus on code and design.
- Be patient with new contributors. Most are volunteers learning as they go.
- Default to public. Discussions in PRs, issues, and the consortium forum
  are the record of how the platform evolved.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or doxxing of any contributor or election agent.
- Partisan advocacy. The platform is non-partisan; contributions are
  evaluated on technical merit alone.
- Posting or transmitting personal information of agents, observers, or
  voters.
- Attempting to use the codebase or platform for voter manipulation,
  disinformation, or election interference.

## Enforcement

Report violations to **conduct@openballot.ng**. The consortium governance
committee reviews reports and may issue warnings, temporary bans, or
permanent bans. Decisions are recorded (without disclosing reporter
identity) so the community can see how norms are enforced.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant v2.1 with
project-specific additions on partisan neutrality.
